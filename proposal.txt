INTELLIGENT PATIENT SERVICE ROBOT SYSTEM
Backup Project Proposal
________________________________________
Executive Summary
This proposal presents an intelligent stationary service kiosk robot designed to revolutionize patient reception and queue management in Zimbabwean hospitals. The robot proactively engages patients waiting in queues, providing multilingual assistance, preliminary assessments, appointment scheduling, information services, and basic amenities—significantly reducing front desk congestion and wait times while improving patient experience.
________________________________________
Problem Statement
Current Challenges at Zimbabwean Hospitals (e.g., Parirenyatwa Hospital, Mpilo Central Hospital, Sally Mugabe Central Hospital)
When patients arrive at public hospitals in Zimbabwe, they face significant challenges:
Long Queue Times
•	Patients wait 30 minutes to 2+ hours just to reach the front desk
•	Only to discover they need to be redirected elsewhere
•	Peak times (mornings, Mondays) see queues of 50-100+ patients
•	Single front desk staff handling multiple responsibilities
Language Barriers
•	Staff may not speak all of Zimbabwe's 16 official languages
•	Patients struggle to explain symptoms or needs
•	Misunderstandings lead to incorrect referrals
•	Elderly patients and rural visitors particularly affected
Information Bottleneck
•	Simple questions (doctor availability, fees, directions, visiting hours) require front desk assistance
•	Complex inquiries delay everyone in queue
•	Patients don't know which department they need
•	No preliminary symptom assessment before seeing staff
Resource Inefficiency
•	Front desk staff overwhelmed with basic queries
•	Medical professionals interrupted for directional questions
•	Patients sent to wrong departments, creating multiple queues
•	No triage happening during wait time

Patient Frustration
•	Lack of information while waiting
•	Uncertainty about wait times
•	No access to basic needs (water, tissue, directions to facilities)
•	Poor experience leads to dissatisfaction with healthcare system
________________________________________
Proposed Solution
An Intelligent Stationary Patient Service Robot that actively circulates through waiting areas, engaging patients in queues to provide:
✅ Multilingual interface (all 16 Zimbabwean languages)
✅ Preliminary symptom assessment and triage recommendations
✅ Real-time doctor availability and appointment scheduling
✅ Hospital information (fees, services, departments, visiting policies)
✅ Navigation assistance (directions to departments, toilets, pharmacies)
✅ Basic amenities (water, tissues, hand sanitizer dispensing)
✅ Patient admission inquiries (check on admitted relatives)
✅ Medication availability checking
✅ Queue management (virtual queue tickets, estimated wait times)

System Architecture
1. Stationary Robot Platform
Physical Design Characteristics:
•	Height: 140-160cm (eye level for seated and standing patients)
•	Base: Stable, wide base (60-70cm diameter) for safety
•	Design: Friendly, approachable appearance (healthcare-themed colors)
•	Touchscreen: Large 15-17 inch display at adjustable height
•	Audio: Speakers for voice announcements and multilingual responses
•	Dispensing Compartments: For water cups, tissues, hand sanitizer
•	Emergency Button: Visible stop button for safety
Mobility Features:
•	Autonomous navigation through waiting areas
•	Differential drive or omnidirectional wheels
•	Slow, safe speed (0.3-0.5 m/s in crowded areas)
•	Approaches patients respectfully, maintains appropriate distance
•	Returns to charging dock when battery low

Use Case Scenarios
Scenario 1: Elderly Patient with Limited English
Profile: Gogo Ncube, 68 years old, speaks only Ndebele, experiencing chest pain
Robot Interaction:
1.	Robot approaches: "Sawubona Gogo, ngingakusiza?" (Hello grandmother, can I help you?)
2.	Gogo explains symptoms in Ndebele via voice
3.	Robot recognizes urgency keywords: chest pain + age
4.	IMMEDIATE ACTION: Robot displays in large text and speaks: 
o	"Lokhu kungaba yingozi. Ngizakubiza umuntu ozakunceda." (This could be serious. I'll call someone to help you.)
5.	Robot sends alert to emergency staff with location
6.	Staff arrives within 2 minutes
7.	Robot provides Gogo with water while waiting
8.	Emergency staff take her to emergency department
Outcome: Potential cardiac event identified and addressed immediately, possibly saving life.
